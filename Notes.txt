Aracha = crude steamed tea leaves including stems/veins (bulk material)

Tencha = refined, stem/vein removed, dried flat leaves from aracha specifically grown for matcha

Matcha = powdered tea ground from tencha leaves

Harvested tea leaves → steaming → aracha (rough steamed leaves) → de-stemming/de-veining + drying flat → tencha → grinding → matcha powder


1. Aracha (粗茶)
Meaning: Literally "rough tea" or "crude tea."

What it is: This is the raw, unrefined green tea leaves after the initial harvest and steaming process but before any sorting, de-stemming, or polishing.

Form: Whole steamed leaves, including stems and veins.

Use: Aracha is like the bulk green tea leaf material that can be further processed into various types of teas including tencha and sencha.

2. Tencha (碾茶)
Meaning: "Stone ground tea" (though actually it refers to the type of leaf and processing before grinding).

What it is: Tencha is made by taking aracha and removing stems and veins (de-stemming and de-veining), then drying the leaves flat without rolling or shaping them.

Form: Flattened, de-stemmed, de-veined tea leaves.

Use: This is the specific type of leaf used exclusively to make matcha powder. It is not rolled or twisted like sencha leaves; it's flat and easy to grind finely.

3. Matcha (抹茶)
Meaning: "Powdered tea."

What it is: Matcha is the finely ground powder made from tencha leaves.

Form: Bright green fine powder.

Use: Whisked in hot water to make the traditional tea, used in tea ceremonies, and in modern culinary applications.
